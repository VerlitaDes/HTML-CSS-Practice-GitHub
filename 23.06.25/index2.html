UI/UX design is not just about how things look — it's about how they work and feel for the user.

Good design helps users navigate a product easily and achieve their goals.
Bad design leads to confusion and frustration.

UX (User Experience) focuses on the overall feel of the experience, while UI (User Interface) is concerned with visual elements and layout.

Considering user needs is essential in the design process — even the smallest details can make a big difference.

Skipping testing is a common mistake made by beginners.

Designers often use UIG to ensure consistency and usability across their products.
